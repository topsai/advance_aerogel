Development of aerogel industry



Aerogel is a new material with nano porous structure. In 1931, it was first created by S.Kistler, an American scientist. At the early stage of its invention, it was applied to toothpaste and cosmetics at first. Because its light mist and blue color, aerogel is also known as "blue smoke" and "frozen smoke". After many years of research and exploration by scientists from France, Germany and other countries, aerogels have developed from Silicon series, Carbon series and Sulphur series to metal series, and their applicatiopre have been greatly expanded. In 1993, aerogels were applied to Aeronautics and Astronautics field by NASA. At present, it has been widely used in thermal insulation, clothing, metal modification and environmental treatment and other multi-purpose, broad applications. Aerogels has been named as the top ten new materials in the new century. However, the further development of aerogels is restricted due to the fact that the manufacturing process is complex and the scale production is not big enough, resulting in high price and insufficient output and limiting its application in the market.

In recent years, the Hongda Technology Team (China) has developed a process of phased decompression, which is different from the traditional method of usinng supercritical drying to produce aerogels. This new process of phased decompression reduces the difficulty of mass production and greatly reduces the price of products without sacrificing the quality of the product. This technology has obtained many patents from the United States and other countries. Aerogels manufactured by this new technology has a density of only 22Kg per cubic meter. Nanapores distributed on the surface of aerogels can be seen under electron microscope. This technology provides a possibility for aerogels to be widely applied in various industries.
