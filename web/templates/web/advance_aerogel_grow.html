Development of aerogel industry



Aerogel is a new material with nano porous structure. In 1931, it was made by S.Kistler, an American scientist. It was applied to toothpaste and cosmetics at first, because it was light mist, blue color, and also known as "blue smoke" and "frozen smoke". After many years of research and exploration by scientists from France, Germany and other countries, aerogels developed from Silicon, Carbon and Sulphur to many kinds of metal systems, and their applications were greatly expanded. In 1993, aerogels were applied to Aeronautics and Astronautics by NASA. At present, it has been widely used in thermal insulation, clothing, metal modification and environmental treatment and other multi-purpose, broad prospects for development, known as the top ten new materials in the new century. However, the further development of aerogels is due to the fact that the manufacturing process is complex and the scale production is not big enough, resulting in high price and insufficient output, and the market application has been limited.

In recent years, the Hongda Technology Team (China) has developed a process of reducing pressure, which is different from the traditional method of supercritical production of aerogels, which reduces the difficulty of mass production and greatly reduces the price of products. The technology has obtained many patents from the United States and other countries, and the quality of gas gel products is excellent. Recently, the product test has only 22Kg per cubic meter. The nano pore materials are of type A. It provides a new choice for aerogels to be widely applied in various industries.
