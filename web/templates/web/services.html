OUR TECHNOLOGY

HONG HITECH developed a totally new technology to manufacture aerogel material. Aerogel is commonly made from a process called supercritical drying where silica gel is combined with a solvent and go through a high pressure and high temperature environment, which causes the solvent to evaporate without collapsing the structure of the gel. This process is costly and slow.




Our process for manufacturing silica aerogel is markedly different from this common process. We use a worldwide patented technology called phased decompression. Instead of using high pressure and high temperature, we gradually lower the pressure while maintaining the structural integrity of the gel. This technology is patented in US, Europe, Japan and China.




We Can Help You

Our technology provides the means to manufacture high quality aerogel at a faster pace and a lower cost. Our aerogel is lightweight and has a good surface to weight ratio. Because of our low cost compared to other suppliers, we can pass on the savings to our customers.

2. Aerogel insulating blanket:


Aerogel insulating blanket is a fiber mesh sheet coated with aerogel liquid. It has the best thermal insulation property, which makes it an ideal insulation wrapping material used for oil industry, the cryo industry (such as LNG shipping, piping), the power industry, the building industry etc.





Hong Hitech has developed another patented technology that makes our aerogel insulating blanket sheet superior to other similar products. Traditionally, the coating of the aerogel onto fiber mesh sheet is done by submerging the sheet into the coating liquid, and then take it out and heated to dry. However, there are some major quality issues associated with products made by this process. As the blanket bends or being exposed in the rain and sunlight, the coating will peel out, which leads to the decrease of thermal protection and the necessity of adding new blankets or replacing the defective blankets.

Our technology uses high vacuum pressure to suck the aerogel into the fiber sheet and mix it with the fiber. This process has made our product perform better than others as they will hardly peel off and are not easy to age, greatly reducing the chance to add new blankets or replacing old ones. The customers could not only enjoy longer thermal insulating time but also save some cost.
