Aerogel is one of the best new materials in the world. Developed by NASA, aerogel has been applied to commercial use in the last couple of decades.

Aerogel belongs to a class of ultralow density solids that combine disparate and extreme materials properties into a single material envelope. Aerogel generally exhibits high porosity, high specific surface area, and superlative energy damping (thermal, acoustic, and impact) properties.

Silica aerogels are a common type of aerogel that combine ultralow thermal conductivity (as low as 10 mW m-1 K-1) with extremely low density (as low as 0.001 g cm-3), high specific surface area (500-2000 m2 g-1), low dielectric constant (as low as 1.02), and good optical transparency (~91% per cm), making them uniquely valuable as high-performance insulators, supports for chemical sensors and catalysts, and particle detectors.
